Dance rehearsals are tedious.
Running to and fro during rehearsals to play, pause, or seek track, is a hassle.
Tempo takes the tedious out of rehearsals.
Tempo is an iOS app to assist dancers with their rehearsals.
  • Import playlists from Spotify, Apple Music or Youtube.
  • Split track into sections.
  • Use voice commands to play, pause or seek track.
To deliver flawless performances, dancers rehearse meticulously.

Thought Process

Coming from a family of professional dancers, I decided to tackle a problem they experience.
Initial Assumption Dancers have a hard time connecting with each other
Magical Solution A social platform
However, after gorging through user research I discovered that dancers do not have this problem. Facebook and Instagram have already covered that.
After speaking to more dancers, it helped me understand that their problems vary from one to another. I was confused and didn’t know where else to look.

Vitamin or Painkiller

Professional Dancers put a lot of time and effort into rehearsals. take their rehearsals seriously. As most performers are extremely busy, they try to make the most of their rehearsal time together. They want to improve the overall quality of performance.
I wanted the solution to be a painkiller. A vitamin is something nice to have, a good supplement just like social media. A painkiller is what dancers will reach out for when they are stressed.

The Hidden Pain Point

I did not find my problem through user research, it actually required careful observation of the dancer’s day-to-day activity. A major waste factor was when the instructor had to stop dance practice to interact with the music player.
During rehearsals, steps are taught or learned in blocks, this is the best way that dancers absorb the routine.
Challenging sections of a song may be learned at a slower pace. Professional performers will rehearse this part repetitively to perfection.

Research

Currently, there are several on-demand streaming services available for music. Music apps such as: Spotify, Deezer, and Soundcloud are ruling the market. However, none of these apps have a voice command feature. One such app that did have this feature was Soundhound.
Soundhound did well with introducing voice commands, and allowing users to access their Spotify playlist. However, there were several complaints on the performance and UI. I decided to take this into consideration while building the prototypes.
Keeping the initial solution in mind, I thought...
What could further ease the rehearsal process?
Based on the book Talent is Overrated by Geoff Colvin, what separates good performers and world-class performers lies in how they practice. Good performers practice the entire routine repeatedly while world-class performers identify and focus their practice in the areas they are weak in.
Dancers who emulate the habit of focusing their practice towards the challenging parts of the routine end up spending a significant amount of time adjusting the music player.

Introducing Tempo

Tempo is a music assistant for dancers and choreographers. It allows them to use voice commands and segment their tracks, so they can completely focus on their rehearsals.
Users can directly import their rehearsal tracks from Spotify, Youtube or Apple Music playlists.
Voice commands can be used to play, pause, rehearsal track. Suggestions for voice commands are available for users that need assistance.
Users can mark a specific section of the track that they wish to rehearse multiple times and cue it at the time of practice. The view is also available in the music player mode.

Branding

The vision for Tempo was to be an assistant to dancers, and not a typical on-demand music streaming service. I had to remind myself of this consistently throughout the journey of this project as I wanted to add more features that doesn't address the problems I've identified.
My inspiration for colours came from a concert I attended.
I wanted to use dark tones with hints of neon glow to bring a modern dance feel.
It didn’t take too long to come up with a name.
Dance + Music
Tempo is a term used in both.
However, the logo was a challenge. I was trying to keep it close to the brand and not make it too detailed. It should be something people will easily remember and relate to, similar to Sony and Spotify.

User Flows

I had several iterations of the user flow, and revisions were consistently done throughout this project. It was important that the flow would not confuse the user. I specifically wanted a smooth onboarding, to train the user to be comfortable and not thrown into darkness. My onboarding handholds the user through their first experience.

Wireframes

I maximized the use of paper prototypes and used sticky notes as drop down menus. It was quick, easy to iterate and test with users. After several user tests, feedback and revisions, I finally decided to push things forward into hi-fidelity.

Failures

  • I first had 3 features on the bottom navigation bar. However, it seemed redundant and confusing to have a separate player and marker tab. It would make more sense to access the marker tool while the track is playing. This was minimized to to 2 icons.
  • The marker tool was originally meant to be a splice. Discussing with my mentor (Ahmad), I later understood that with a splice tool, the problem of organizing splices would arise. Hence, I used inspiration from an app, Traktr, and decided on a marker tool that will allow the users to cue their music

Future Considerations
